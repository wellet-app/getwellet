The coordination burden

You're the one who holds it all. No tool was built for that.

VA caregivers carry a specific, exhausting kind of load — and until now, no tool was designed for the person doing the actual work.

The coordination weight

Your Veteran's health information lives inside the VA system. You're the person doing the day-to-day coordination — the one who remembers the last lab result, the exact medication dose, which specialist said what. That's not a small job. Wellet stays on top of it with you.

The credential reality

The VA hasn't built a caregiver proxy system yet. In practice, most caregivers download the VA Health Summary using their family member's VA.gov login — or the Veteran downloads it themselves and hands it over. Wellet doesn't require anything else. Download the file, upload it here.

The outside appointment gap

VA records don't travel well. When your family member sees an outside specialist — a cardiologist, an orthopedist, a community neurologist — no one in that room has the VA's data. You're the bridge. Wellet makes sure you always have what they need.

How it works

Four steps. About ten minutes. Done.

No VA approval required. No API setup. No waiting. The VA Health Summary is a file your family member can download right now — and Wellet reads every section of it.

1

Sign in at VA.gov/my-health

Go to VA.gov/my-health and sign in with Login.gov or ID.me. The old MyHealtheVet URL (myhealth.va.gov) now redirects there automatically.

2

Go to Medical Records

Select My Health in the top navigation, then choose Medical records from the left sidebar or the dashboard card.

3

Download the VA Health Summary

Look for VA Health Summary — not "Blue Button Report," which is a different format. Select Download VA Health Summary and choose the XML format when prompted. The PDF works too, but XML gives Wellet the most complete picture.

4

Upload to Wellet — done in 10 seconds

Inside Wellet, tap Add VA Health Summary, select the file (it'll be named something like VA_Health_Summary.xml), and Wellet reads it immediately. Your family member's timeline populates on the spot.

No password sharing needed: If your Veteran has an iPhone, Wellet Connect can sync health data automatically — no need to share VA.gov credentials. The CCDA upload path above works for anyone, regardless of device.

What Wellet reads

Everything in the VA Health Summary, organized.

The VA Health Summary is a structured XML document — Wellet reads every clinical section and turns it into a timeline you can actually use.

Conditions

The full problem list with onset dates where available — so you can walk into any appointment with a complete picture of what's been diagnosed.

Medications

The active medication list with dosage and indication. When a new prescription comes in at an outside appointment, the full list is right there.

Allergies

Substance, reaction type, and severity. Critical information that often doesn't make it from the VA chart to an outside provider's intake form.

Immunizations

Vaccine names and administration dates — useful for community providers, travel medicine, and care transitions.

Lab Results

Panel names, result values, and dates. Wellet reads each result into the timeline so you can follow the trend across visits — not just the most recent number.

Vital Signs

The most recent readings — blood pressure, weight, BMI, and more. Useful context at any appointment, not just VA visits.

Procedures

Surgical and clinical procedures with dates — the kind of history that matters when a new specialist asks what's already been tried.

Point-in-time snapshot

The VA Health Summary reflects the Veteran's record as of the download date. It doesn't update automatically — but re-uploading a fresh copy from VA.gov takes less than two minutes, and Wellet merges it with what's already there.

Your data

Private by design. Yours to control.

Health data is not a product. Wellet treats it like the sensitive, personal information it is.

Encrypted and isolated

Every record is stored encrypted and isolated with row-level security — scoped to your account, inaccessible to other users. The raw VA Health Summary file is stored in a private bucket with the same protection.

You own it

You control who sees your family member's data. You can export everything or delete it anytime — no hoops, no support tickets. The data is yours, not Wellet's.

Never trained on

Your health data is never used to train AI models, shared with advertisers, or sold to third parties. It exists in Wellet for one purpose: to help you coordinate care.

No VA approval required — this path uses a file the Veteran has already downloaded from their own VA.gov account. Wellet never accesses VA systems directly.
